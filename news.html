Official launch of the SHELTER programme
(Interreg Caraïbes)

The Caribbean Network of Researchers on Sickle Cell Disease and Thalassemia (CAREST) officially launched the SHELTER programme, bringing together its partners around a clear and shared objective: Strengthening the fight against sickle cell disease in the Caribbean by improving access to care and developing regional research programmes.

This launch marks an important milestone for regional collaboration and commitment to patients and research innovation.

Congratulations and many thanks to our colleagues in Pointe-à-Pitre, whose strong involvement and dedication play a key role in the success of the CAREST network.

A New doctor from our laboratory

Léa Kuznicki successfully defended her doctoral thesis on Thursday, December 4th”

Her work investigated the effects of hydroxyurea on the regulation of erythropoiesis in sickle cell disease.

We were deeply impressed by the high scientific level of discussion she achieved with her examining committee.

Congratulations, Léa! The BaHSic team is very proud of you!

A New doctor from our laboratory

Alisa Soudry, PhD student in our laboratory, has brilliantly defended her thesis entitled “Characterization of new blood group antigens and their functions in blood cells.”

She delivered a clear and compelling presentation of her outstanding results and explained the successive steps required to discover new blood groups.

Congratulations, Alisa! The BaHSic team is very proud of you!

🗓️ November 12, 2025

Medals of Involvement Ceremony – Université Paris Cité

On September 16, 2025, the first ceremony for the awarding of the Medals of Involvement of Université Paris Cité took place.

This distinction aims to recognize and celebrate colleagues who have contributed in an exemplary manner to the influence of the University, whether through their work in research, innovation, teaching, or institutional engagement.

On this occasion, our colleague and former director, Caroline Le Van Kim, was honored for her remarkable career, marked by constant and exceptional dedication to research and education.

We are proud to count Caroline among us and grateful to benefit every day from her experience and expertise.

Congratulations Caroline, a well-deserved recognition!
